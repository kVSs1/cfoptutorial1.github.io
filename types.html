Types of CFOP

There are two types of CFOP: Beginner CFOP and Advanced CFOP. The main difference between the two is the number of algorithms (algs). An algorithm is a set of moves/turns done to achieve something. In Beginner Method, there are less algorithms so that it is easier to learn.

There are total 16 algorithms in Beginner CFOP, 10 for OLL and 6 for PLL. The Cross and F2L are intuitive, which means it does not require any algorithms. 16 Algorithms seem small if you compare it to the 79 Algorithms for Advanced CFOP (not including the extra 42 algorithms for most efficient F2L solving)! There are 58 OLL Algorithms and 21 PLL Algorithms in Advanced CFOP.

Another difference is the efficiency. Beginner CFOP, being more intuitive is less efficient than Advanced CFOP. Efficiency over here includes number of turns, your TPS (Turns Per Second), and how much you need to think and wait in between to see (muscle memory). Advanced CFOP has less turns, high average TPS, and more muscle memory (due to learning the case and the algorithm required) than Beginner CFOP.

To conclude the differences, if you want to learn less algorithms and still get good enough times without straining alot, you should use Beginner CFOP. But if you want to get better times and go to competitions at national/international levels, you should use Advanced CFOP. It is up to you what you want to learn.
