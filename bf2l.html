Beginner F2L

F2L stands for First 2 Layers (First Two Layers). It is the process of solving both the first, and the second layer of the Rubik's Cube together. To do this, we need to pair the white corner and the corresponding edge together, and then inserting it into its respective slot. A slot is the place where an F2L pair is to be put. The main purpose is the pair a corner piece and an edge piece into an easy insert.

There are two types of easy inserts:

Lefty Insert

Righty Insert

Lefty Insert

The Lefty Insert requires the following set of moves:

U' L' U L (or inverse lefty algorithm/inverse lefty sexy algorithm)

Righty Insert

The Righty Insert requires the following algorithm:

U R U' R' (or inverse righty/inverse sexy algorithm)

The above algorithms require the pair to be just above the slot in such a way that the corner piece of the pair is just above the corner piece where it is supposed to go.

All the other algorithms are used to make an easy insert.

The other cases are:

When the corner piece and the edge piece are seperated, different colours are on top, and white is facing to the right side, hold the cube such that the corner piece is on top of the slot and the white side faces right and not front and do the following algorithm (Remember, the corner piece and edge piece should have the same colours) :

R U R'

For its mirror case (when different colours are on top, white is facing left side, and the corner is above the top and you are holding it such that the white side is facing left and not front), do the following:

L' U' L
